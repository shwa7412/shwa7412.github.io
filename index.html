About

I am Shrey Waghmare, a dynamic engineer pursuing a Master's (Thesis) in Mechanical Engineering at CU Boulder, specializing in thermofluids. With over 4 years of experience at the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO), I have a strong foundation in design, development and analysis of mechanical systems.

During my time at ISRO, I've contributed to more than 10+ successful missions by leading the design and analysis of rocket engine components. My work ranged from numerical modelling and multiphysics simulations of engine hardware to component assembly, subsystem integration, and final product depolyment.

Currently at CU Boulder, I am enhancing my expertise in Machine Learning and Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD). My thesis focuses on utilizing machine learning models for the identification of combustion-relevant species from their infrared spectra.

With a strong foundation in Aerospace and Mechanical Engineering and growing expertise in Machine Learning, I am driven to contribute to the development of high-efficiency mechanical systems, leveraging advanced computational techniques and innovative engineering solutions.
